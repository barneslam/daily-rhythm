## Caption

You don't get paid for what you fix.
You get paid for what was agreed.

And those are rarely the same thing.

I used to go beyond the brief — connecting gaps, improving systems, making execution actually work.

Better system. Worse alignment.

Because I crossed a line:
From delivering work → to taking ownership
Without resetting the agreement.

That's where most execution breaks.

Not from lack of effort.
But from lack of system definition.

That's the shift I've made:

I either deliver what's scoped
Or I stop and redesign the system properly.

No in-between.

Strategy doesn't fail.

Execution systems do.
