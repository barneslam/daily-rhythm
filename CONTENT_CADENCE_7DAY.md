## MONDAY: Opportunity Leakage Pain + Recovery Solution

### Theme
**"The deals you've already lost this month"**

### Core Message
Founders and business owners have revenue sitting in their inboxes right now. Customers they've already talked to, proposals they've sent, conversations that started but stopped. The cost isn't in finding new customers — it's in the opportunities already sitting, dormant, waiting for one more touch.

### Pain Points to Trigger
1. **Stalled proposals** — Sent proposal → No follow-up → Lost deal
2. **Warm inbound** — Customer inquiry → Got busy → Never responded
3. **Customer conversations** — Talked about solving their problem → Disappeared from view
4. **Referral introductions** — Someone said "yes, interested" → It's been 2 weeks
5. **Closed/lost deals** — Deal fell through 3 months ago → Never circled back

### Commercial Hook
"If you closed even ONE deal from your inbox this month instead of chasing new cold leads, how much would that be worth?"

### Emotional Connection
The guilt of seeing that email and thinking "I should have followed up." The frustration of losing deals you already had momentum on. The wasted energy chasing new leads while ignoring the ones who already said yes.

### Key Copy Themes
- **"The deals you've already lost"** — Specific, painful, measurable
- **"Sitting in your inbox right now"** — Immediate, urgent, tangible
- **"One more touch"** — Low effort, high impact
- **"Revenue you already have access to"** — It's not new sales, it's recovery

### Visual Requirements
- Real founder or operator sitting at laptop
- Laptop screen showing email inbox OR CRM with visible opportunities
- Honest facial expression (not forced, not staged)
- Props: coffee, maybe second monitor, real desk environment
- Executive/professional visual tone

### Sample Copy (LinkedIn)
```
Found 4 deals in my inbox yesterday that I stopped following up on.

$12K in proposals. All from people who said "interested."

Didn't lose them to a competitor. Lost them to my own follow-up breakdown.

One more message each. That's it.

Message RECOVER if you know the feeling.
```

### Sample Copy (Instagram)
```
The revenue you didn't chase.

(It was already in your inbox.)

Your next customer may already know your name.
Message RECOVER to reclaim what's yours.
```

### CTA
**Primary:** "Message RECOVER"
- Direct, actionable, mysterious (creates curiosity)
- Leads to conversation about the Sprint

**Soft:** "Your next customer may already know your name"
- Hooks into the central insight
- Works in captions, comments

### Metrics to Track
- Reach (LinkedIn impressions, IG reach)
- Engagement (replies, saves, shares)
- Direct messages with "RECOVER" keyword
- Conversations initiated

## TUESDAY: Wasted Lead Generation Spend / Missed Opportunities

### Theme
**"The leads you're paying for while ignoring the ones you have"**

### Core Message
Many founders and agencies are spending thousands on lead generation (ads, tools, outreach) while sitting on a goldmine of warm leads from past conversations, referrals, and inbound inquiries. The math doesn't work: expensive new leads vs. free warm conversations.

### Pain Points to Trigger
1. **Ad spend inefficiency** — $5K/month on ads for 0.5% conversion
2. **Lead generation cost** — Tools, services, automation → Low ROI
3. **Unused inbound** — People who came to you → You didn't follow up
4. **Referral pile-up** — Introductions that got lost → Never contacted them
5. **Opportunity cost** — Chasing new while losing the warm

### Commercial Hook
"If your CAC is $500 but you have 20 warm leads sitting ignored, what are you doing paying for ads?"

### Emotional Connection
The frustration of seeing the ad spend adding up while results don't materialize. The embarrassment of losing deals to simple follow-up gaps. The cognitive dissonance of "growth" talk while the easy revenue is right there.

### Key Copy Themes
- **"The leads you're already paying for"** — They exist, you've invested, you're ignoring them
- **"Your highest-ROI leads"** — Warm > cold, always
- **"Cost per acquisition vs. cost per relationship"** — Different equations
- **"One more touch"** — Minimal investment, maximum upside

### Visual Requirements
- Operator/consultant at laptop with CRM or email visible
- Screen showing: high lead count, low interaction
- Real business setting, honest expression
- Props: maybe a credit card, analytics dashboard visible
- Professional but slightly stressed (relatable)

Message RECOVER to flip the equation.
```

### CTA
**Primary:** "Message RECOVER"

**Soft:** "Your CAC through warm follow-up is free. Your next customer already knows your name."

But proposals that don't get closed usually just fade — no automated follow-up, no escalation, just a cold email sitting in someone's inbox. ### Pain Points to Trigger
1. **Proposal sent → Radio silence** — No follow-up cadence
2. **"Let me think about it"** → Three weeks of nothing
3. **Budget cycles** → "Will revisit in Q3" → Forgotten by Q3
4. **Stakeholder changes** → Proposal was with the wrong person
5. **Timing issues** → Right solution, wrong time → Lost in inbox

"How many proposals are sitting in people's inboxes right now that are still valid?"

## FRIDAY: Follow-Up Breakdown + Recovery Rhythm

### Theme
**"The one thing that kills more deals than anything else"**

### Core Message
It's not about being the smartest or having the best product. It's about showing up repeatedly. Most founders and operators don't have a daily follow-up rhythm. They chase new leads, hope something sticks, and wonder why their conversion rates suck. The sprint is about installing a daily practice of staying in touch with warm conversations.

### Pain Points to Trigger
1. **No follow-up system** — It's random, reactive, inconsistent
2. **Busy syndrome** → Follow-up is the first thing to drop
3. **CRM abandoned** — Leads go in, no process gets them back out
4. **Shame around follow-up** → Feels pushy, so they don't do it
5. **Feast or famine cycles** → No pipeline, so new business chasing is desperate

### Commercial Hook
"Most founders fail at sales because they have no daily follow-up rhythm. Not because they're bad at closing."

### Emotional Connection
The frustration of inconsistency. The shame of being "that person" who disappears. The exhaustion of always chasing new leads because you never systematized the warm ones. The knowledge that it's simple but the inability to make it stick.

### Key Copy Themes
- **"It's not complicated, it's just a rhythm"** — Daily, consistent, repeatable
- **"Show up for the conversations that are already warm"** — That's the whole thing
- **"The deal is lost in the in-between"** — Between conversations, time passes, momentum dies
- **"One more touch per day per lead"** — Small habit, massive compound effect

### Visual Requirements
- Founder at laptop, actively working (not thinking, doing)
- Inbox visible, calendar visible, real workflow
- Sense of motion/productivity
- Props: coffee, multiple screens, notes
- Focused, in-the-flow expression

## SATURDAY: Short Case-Style Lesson

### Theme
**"How [Founder] recovered [$ amount] in stalled deals in [timeframe]"**

### Core Message
Social proof through real-world recovery. Show that it's possible to reactivate dormant conversations and win back revenue. Anonymized but specific (with permission). Focuses on the process and the outcome, not motivation.

### Structure
1. **Situation** — The problem they faced (20+ stalled deals, no follow-up system)
2. **Action** — The specific moves they made (3-day focused recovery sprint)
3. **Result** — The specific outcome ($45K in revived pipeline, 8 reactivated conversations)
4. **Lesson** — The insight (opportunity recovery isn't hard, it's just forgotten)

### Pain Points to Address
- Proof that recovery is real
- Specific, measurable results
- Relatability (founder just like them)
- Process-focused (not luck)

### Copy Approach
- **Keep it short** (visual-first content)
- **Lead with the outcome** (result first, story second)
- **No fluff** (just facts)
- **Real numbers** (specific, credible)

### Visual Requirements
- Real founder/operator (if they consent)
- Before/after visual (inbox screenshots, proposal count, whatever changed)
- Professional documentation feel
- Authentic, not staged

### Sample Copy (LinkedIn)
```
In 48 hours, Sarah recovered $45K in stalled deals.

Situation: 24 open proposals, zero follow-up system.

Action: Spent Friday + Saturday contacting everyone with "I want to revisit this."

Result: 8 conversations reopened, 3 now moving forward.

Lesson: Opportunity recovery isn't about being smarter. It's about showing back up.

1. **Pressure to always be closing** → But growth is actually rhythm
2. **Guilt around follow-up** → Feels pushy, so people don't do it (wrong logic)
3. **Imposter syndrome in sales** → "I'm not a salesperson" (most successful founders aren't)
4. **Feast-or-famine stress** → Could be solved with one simple practice
5. **Loneliness of founder** → Selling is vulnerable, and it's usually solo

### Emotional Connection
This is the moment where the founder shares a personal realization or truth about their own journey. Not motivational, not preachy — just honest.

### Copy Approach
- **Personal story** (something real from your experience)
- **The contrarian take** (sales is about showing up, not being great at closing)
- **The vulnerability** (what you got wrong, what you learned)
- **The insight** (simple truth you'd like to share)
- **No CTA** (this is trust-building, not converting)

- Not smiling (not forced)
- Real environment, real moment
- Honest, not polished
- Could be photo or honest prose-only post

## CONTENT EXECUTION FRAMEWORK

### Weekly Workflow

**Friday (Planning):**
1. Review analytics from previous week
2. Identify top-performing theme (which day resonated most?)
3. Outline next week's content
4. Source or plan visual assets
5. Draft captions for all 7 days

**Saturday:**
1. Finalize copy for Mon-Wed
2. Curate/source images
3. Format for both LinkedIn + Instagram
4. Schedule Mon-Tue posts

**Sunday:**
1. Finalize copy for Thu-Fri
2. Final image selection
3. Schedule Wed-Thu posts
4. Outline Sat/Sun content

**Mon-Fri (Execution):**
1. Monitor post engagement in real-time
2. Respond to comments + DMs immediately
3. Track "RECOVER" messages
4. Nurture conversations (move people toward call/signup)
5. Take notes on what resonates for next week

---

### Copy Writing Formula

Every post follows this structure:

1. **Hook** (first 2 lines) — Problem or insight that's immediately relatable
2. **Context** (3-5 lines) — Specific situation or pain point
3. **Insight** (2-3 lines) — The realization or contrarian point
4. **Proof** (optional, 1-2 lines) — A number or real example
5. **CTA** (final line) — Soft, curious, action-oriented

**Example:**
```
[HOOK] "Found 4 deals in my inbox yesterday."

[CONTEXT] "All from people who said interested. Didn't lose them to a competitor."

[INSIGHT] "Lost them to follow-up breakdown."

[PROOF] "$12K in proposals sitting there."

[CTA] "Message RECOVER if you know the feeling."
```

---

### Visual Asset Requirements

For each day's theme, source or create:
- **Primary image** (LinkedIn 1200x627, IG 1080x1080)
- **Instagram Story variant** (1080x1920)
- **Real-person imagery only**
  - ✓ Founder at laptop (honest expression, real environment)
  - ✓ Consultant in meeting (real conversation moment)
  - ✓ Agency owner reviewing CRM (focus, concentration)
  - ✓ Service business owner on call (listening, engaged)
  - ✓ Operator thinking/planning (alone, authentic)
  
- **NO:**
  - AI avatars or AI-generated imagery
  - Cartoons or illustrations
  - Generic stock photos (shaking hands, hugging, laughing at lunch)
  - Fake "startup life" aesthetics
  - Overly posed or staged photography

### Image Library Strategy

Build a library of 5-10 key people:
1. **Founder A** — In different business contexts (laptop, meeting, alone thinking)
2. **Founder B** — Same approach
3. **Consultant** — Client meetings, email, CRM visible
4. **Agency owner** — Team context, laptop context
5. **Service operator** — One-person shop energy, authentic

**Shoot them once, use them across the entire cadence** for consistency.

### A/B Testing Framework

Test one variable per week:

**Week 1:** Baseline (as described above)
**Week 2:** Visual test (different photography style for same theme)
**Week 3:** Copy test (more aggressive vs. softer emotional tone)
**Week 4:** CTA test (different call-to-action language)

Track which wins and optimize.

### Platform-Specific Formatting

**LinkedIn:**
- Full captions (LinkedIn rewards longer posts)
- Professional tone, credibility focus
- CTAs more explicit ("Message RECOVER")
- Use hashtags strategically (#FollowUp #Sales #Founder)
- Encourage comments/conversation

**Instagram:**
- Short captions (2-3 sentences max)
- Image is 80% of the message
- CTAs softer, more creative ("Your next customer may already know your name")
- Use Stories for engagement (polls, questions)
- Reels for motion (if doing case study or how-to)

**Both:**
- Publish at 9 AM EST (optimal engagement)
- Respond to every comment in first hour
- Engage with similar content from others
- No emojis (professional, commercial tone)
